Seahorse

Seahorses are mainly found in shallow tropical and temperate waters throughout the world, from about 45°S to 45°N [4] and live in sheltered areas such as seagrass beds, estuaries, coral reefs, or mangroves.

  • Scientific Name: Hippocampus
  • Seahorses range in size: from 1.5 to 35.5 cm
  • Average Lifespan: 1 - 5 Years
  • Habitat: shallow tropical and temperate waters throughout the world

Seahorse is the name given to 54 species of small marine fishes in the genus Hippocampus. "Hippocampus" comes from the Ancient Greek word hippos meaning "horse" and kampos meaning "sea monster".[2] The word "seahorse" is often written as two separate words (sea horse), or hyphenated (sea-horse).[3] Having a head and neck suggestive of a horse, seahorses also feature segmented bony armour, an upright posture and a curled prehensile tail.
